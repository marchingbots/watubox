- Limited Partnership

- TWO PARTNERSHIP TYPES:

a. Active Partners (sekutu aktif): manage the business w/ personal liability

b. Limited Partners (sekutu komanditer): invest capital but do not manage operations and have limited liability.

OWNERSHIP:

- Foreign ownership not permitted.
A civil partnership based on agreement between two or more people to contribute something (money, goods or labour) with the intent of sharing profits.

ESTABLISHMENT REQUIREMENTS:

- minimum of 2 partners
- written and notarized agreement (can also be oral)
- optional registration with MOLHR
- no capital requirement
- no specific licensing requirements unless doing busines in a regulated sector (in which case OSS licenses apply) - not a legal entity (partnership cannot own assets under its own name)

OWNERSHIP:

- can be owned by indonesian individuals or entities;
- foreigners may participate ONLY IF:

a. partnership does not involve a commercial enterprise; OR

b. it involves sectors that are not restricted under the NIL/Priority List

1. Define commercial enterprise.


a civil partnership (maatschap) is considered a "non-legal business entity" rather than a commercial enterprise because it lacks the legal separation of assets that characterizes legal entities. Specifically, a civil partnership doesn't create a distinct legal entity with its own assets separate from the partners' personal assets.
