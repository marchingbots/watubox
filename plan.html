STRATEGIES
3 Main Elements:
1. Diagnosis - explains the challenges and opportunities
2. Guiding Policy - for dealing with challenges / opportunities;
3. Coherent Actions - to carry out the guiding policy in coordinated steps

1. Diagnosis
Strategy cannot be developed without diagnosing current situation.
What is going on?
- Views: get everyones' view and write them down
- Clients: they provide info thru surveys and interviews
- Consultants: provide facilitation and external view
- Colleagues: advice from other lawyers not competing with you
- Accountant and bank: can provide context;
- Independent 3rd parties: can provide particular focus or skill.

2. Strategy
Devised for dealing with the situation and getting the best outcome for the business.
- what advantages does the competition have?
- should differentiate you from others
Offering same products/services = they become commodities
Commiditised products = diminished value and price
solution = BESPOKE (highly personalised) and COMMODITISED services
Commodity requires streamlined processes, fast turnaround, efficient service, reliance on tech

Differentiation
Guiding Policy and strategy maps differentiation, specialised differentiated position that increases value of business.


3. Coherent Actions
Describes exactly what you are going to do about the situation described in the Strategy.
- actions must be coherent: consistent with guiding policy and focused.
- actions must be applied and taken consistently by everyone in the business
- compliance by all parties is key


MEASURED
coherent actions should be measurable and must be measured.
- how do you know you achieved your goals?
- devise indicators for performance of "soft" outcomes (communication, delegation, cross-selling, etc)
- what are the KPIs?
- benchmarking - comparing your business against other similar business


REGULARLY REVIEWED
No plan should be set in concrete and should be regularly reviewed.
- formal annual review
- one full day, outside the office, no distractions
- any plan changes written into the planning document


OBSERVED BY ALL
The plan must be observed by all to get buy-in.
"WE WILL ALL DO WHAT WE SAY WE WILL DO"
